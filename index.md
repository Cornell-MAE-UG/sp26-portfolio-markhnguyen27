## About Me

![Profile Photo](/assets/images/MARK_NGUYEN_Headshot.jpg)
 
My name is Mark Nguyen, and I am a sophomore Biological Engineering student who sits at the cross roads of STEM and Business Strategy. 

At Cornell University, I balance studies in both a Biological Engineering Degree and Business minor, working towards furthering Biotechnology in both private and public markets. Currently, I am the **Business Team Lead for Cornell iGEM**, where I simultaniously gained exposure and training in lab techniques, process engineering and manufacturing flows, as well as entreprenurial and financial planning for our project startup. I built and managed the financing of the teams $50k R&D and competition budget: writing proposals, evaluating IP, and transforming experimental data into a viable commerical framework. In lab,I assisted in the model organism transformation of *E. Coli* and the modelling of organism success. At the desk, I assisted in the phyiscal construction of a novel bioreactor. In the boardroom, I ran risk analysis, market research, and developed a business model canva. Working on developing my skills in diverse technical fields, I believe that sucess in Biotechnology comes from a deep understanding of all proponents of product to market.

In addition to my work with iGEM, I also work as a **Business Analyst at Cornell Consult Your Community**, providing and delivering data-driven strategies to enhance customer relations and satisfaction for real local clients. My background spans consulting, financial modeling, and stakeholder management, grounded in a quantitative foundation from my engineering coursework.

My goal is to transform the Biotech market into the forefront of technological advance, where Life Science and Technology combine with Financial Literacy to form a diverse and impactful field.
